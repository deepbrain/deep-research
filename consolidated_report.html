Executive Summary

This report was produced by an autonomous multi-agent research pipeline (Market Shift Detection v8.0) that scanned over 500 publicly available URLs across financial news, regulatory filings, industry databases, and academic sources between 14–15 February 2026. Thirty-three Tier 1 discovery agents operating across 17 independent pipeline runs generated 680+ candidate causal chains, of which 11 survived a multi-stage filtering process and are presented here as candidate research hypotheses for further diligence.

Methodology

The pipeline operates in six phases. Phase 1 (Discovery): autonomous agents scan distinct market sectors for anomalies and construct multi-hop causal chains linking triggers to market-relevant outcomes. Phase 1.5 (Deduplication): overlapping or redundant chains are merged. Phase 2 (Verification): eight independent Layer 2 Verifier agents ground each chain against primary sources, assigning corroboration scores. Phase 2.5 (Arbitration): a Cross-Verifier Arbitrator resolves contradictions between verifiers. Phase 2.75 (Instrument Mapping): an Instrument Identification Agent maps each chain to potential exposures and beneficiaries (no trade parameters). Phase 3 (Synthesis): chains are ranked by composite score (NON_OBV × 0.35 + CORR × 0.30 + ORIG × 0.20 + CONF × 0.15) and compiled into this report. Only chains receiving PASS or CONDITIONAL verdicts from the verification stage are included.

Limitations

This report was produced using exclusively freely available resources on the internet. Its conclusions should be treated as starting points for further analysis, not as research hypotheses. The following limitations apply:

  • No proprietary financial data. The pipeline lacks access to Bloomberg, Refinitiv, FactSet, or equivalent terminal-grade data sources. Fundamental data (earnings revisions, positioning, flow data) is sourced from public filings and press coverage, introducing latency and coverage gaps.
  • Limited quantitative rigor. Theses are constructed from qualitative causal reasoning corroborated against public sources. A production system should incorporate quantitative back-testing, factor exposure analysis, and statistical validation of the causal links asserted.
  • Hallucination risk. Large-language-model agents may fabricate or misattribute claims. While the multi-verifier architecture reduces this risk, dedicated hallucination-reduction techniques — retrieval-augmented generation with source-pinned citations, chain-of-verification prompting, and adversarial red-teaming — would materially improve reliability.
  • No fund-specific alignment. Composite scoring weights are generic. A production deployment should employ GRPO (Group Relative Policy Optimisation) model tuning to align agent outputs with a specific fund’s mandate, risk appetite, sector constraints, and investment horizon — ensuring the ranking reflects institutional priorities rather than abstract novelty metrics.
  • No point-in-time data. No point-in-time data was available for system reliability checking.
