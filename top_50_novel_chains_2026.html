Methodology

The pipeline operates in five tiers. Tier 1 (Reading): 33 Reader agents ingest raw research outputs from parallel multi-agent runs, extracting causal chains with quantitative claims, instruments, and source URLs. Tier 2 (Ranking & Deduplication): an Expanded Ranker deduplicates ~110 candidate chains across all readers, applies composite scoring (0.25×Capital + 0.25×Novelty + 0.20×Duration + 0.15×Causal Depth + 0.15×Data Quality), thematic priority bonuses (AI capex +10%, AI infrastructure +10%, second/third-order AI effects +12%, capital redirection +8%, productivity beneficiaries +8%; max 15% cap), cross-run attestation bonuses, and diversification constraints to select the top 50. Tier 3 (Grounding): 6 Grounder agents independently verify every quantitative claim via live web searches, tagging each source as Original [O] or Corroboration [C] with quality ratings (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW). Tier 4 (Synthesis): 2 Writer agents assemble the final HTML with full citation chains, hop trails, instrument metrics, and bear cases. Tier 5 (Validation): automated checks confirm structural integrity (div balance, 50 chain sections, zero placeholder URLs, reference cross-linking). Only chains with PASS or CONDITIONAL verdicts from the grounding stage are included.

Limitations

This report was produced using exclusively freely available resources on the internet. Its conclusions should be treated as starting points for further analysis, not as research hypotheses. The following limitations apply:

  • No proprietary financial data. The pipeline lacks access to Bloomberg, Refinitiv, FactSet, or equivalent terminal-grade data sources. Fundamental data (earnings revisions, positioning, flow data) is sourced from public filings and press coverage, introducing latency and coverage gaps.
  • Limited quantitative rigor. Theses are constructed from qualitative causal reasoning corroborated against public sources. A production system should incorporate quantitative back-testing, factor exposure analysis, and statistical validation of the causal links asserted.
  • Hallucination risk. Large-language-model agents may fabricate or misattribute claims. While the multi-agent grounding pipeline (reader → ranker → grounder) reduces this risk through independent cross-checking, dedicated hallucination-reduction techniques — retrieval-augmented generation with source-pinned citations, chain-of-verification prompting, and adversarial red-teaming — would materially improve reliability.
  • No fund-specific alignment. Composite scoring weights are generic. A production deployment should employ GRPO (Group Relative Policy Optimisation) model tuning to align agent outputs with a specific fund’s mandate, risk appetite, sector constraints, and investment horizon — ensuring the ranking reflects institutional priorities rather than abstract novelty metrics.
  • No point-in-time data. No point-in-time data was available for system reliability checking.
